Crows in ancient history

Crows were used mostly as a symbol of intelligence and love in ancient societies. The two biggest celebrators of this bird are Egypt and Greece. Egypt saw that they were monogamous and used them as the icon of love. They also saw how they would drop nuts from high up onto stone buildings to break nuts as a form of innovation and Intellectual reasoning. Greece thought so fondly of crows that they would even have them represent deities in the form of Appolo the God of prophecy and Athena the Goddess of wisdom. They strove to compare to this strong bird

They suffer from crows ripping into the trash and making messes normally. cities in the United States that use garbage cans and bins find that crows can't get in so they eat the litter cleaning up the streets.

Crows in Mythology

Crows in Native American folklore are viewed as trickster gods similar to coyotes. They are also so well renowned in the native cultures that some are called crow tribes out of reverence for the bird. In Celtic Mythology three crows together are the harbinger of death and the eyes and ears of the god of death. Morrighan the warrior Goddess regularly appears as a crow in many Celtic myths as well. In many fantasy and gothic literature, the crow is what a witch, wizard, or other evil characters will become to evade capture due to the bird actually being difficult to capture in real life.
